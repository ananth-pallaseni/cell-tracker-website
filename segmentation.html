About Microscopy Stacks

A quick introduction to microscopy stacks.

  • All data from this project comes from 4d microscope images of the bone marrow in a mouse's skull.
  • 2d images are taken at varying depths in the marrow to get a 3d snapshot (and creating a "stack" of 2d images).
  • This is repeated at regular intervals to get "stacks" for each time step.

Preprocessing

The stack initially undergoes some basic preprocessing to reduce the noise and threshold the image for further analysis.

Finding Blobs

Given a binary stack, this step connects adjacent bright pixels to form blobs.

The algorithm connects pixels in each individual xy frame, then combines blobs that are above and below each other in the z-direction.

Filtering Shapes

Given a set of blobs, this step then filters them based on their morphological charactersitics.

Tracking

Filtered blobs are tracked across time.

  1. Match Blobs
    For a blob at time t at position p, the next blob in the sequence is the closest blob in time t+1 which is within a given radius of p.
  2. Remove Duplicates
    The tracks are processed so that each blob only appears in one track. If a blob appears in multiple tracks, then it is assigned to the track where its distance to its predecessor is lowest. The other tracks are broken.
  3. Filter Tracks
    Finally, tracks lasting less than 20 timesteps and tracks where blobs show a minimum of movement across their lifetime are removed.
